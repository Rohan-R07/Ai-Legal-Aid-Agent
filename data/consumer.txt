The Consumer Protection Act, 2019, provides robust rights to consumers in India.

Key Rights:
- Right to Safety: Protection from hazardous goods.
- Right to be Informed: About the quality, quantity, purity, standard, and price of goods.
- Right to Choose: Access to a variety of goods at competitive prices.
- Right to be Heard: Consumer's interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.
- Right to Seek Redressal: Against unfair trade practices or exploitation.

Filing a Complaint:
- You can file a complaint online through the E-Daakhil portal.
- Website: https://consumerhelpline.gov.in/
- Office: National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC) for high-value claims. For district-level issues, contact the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum.
- Form: No specific form number, as the complaint is filed as a detailed document, but it must follow the format prescribed by the NCDRC.
