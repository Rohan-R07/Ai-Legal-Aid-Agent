Indian labour laws provide several protections for employees regarding termination.

Key Points:
- Unfair Dismissal: An employer cannot terminate an employee without a valid, proven reason, especially in cases of misconduct.
- Notice Period: Employees are entitled to a notice period or payment in lieu of notice, as specified in their employment contract or the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947.
- Gratuity & Final Settlement: An employee is entitled to gratuity after 5 years of continuous service and a full and final settlement of dues.

Filing a Complaint:
- First, send a legal notice to the employer.
- If unresolved, you can approach the Labour Commissioner's office in your city.
- Office: Office of the Labour Commissioner.
- Website: Varies by state (e.g., for Delhi: http://www.labour.delhigovt.nic.in/).
- Form: Complaint Form (often referred to as Form 'A' or 'C' depending on the state's rules).
