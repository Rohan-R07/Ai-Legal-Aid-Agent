Civil Law (India):

Civil law deals with disputes between individuals or organizations where criminal punishment is not involved.

Common civil matters include:
- Property inheritance and family property disputes
- Rental and landlord-tenant issues
- Small business and contract-related disputes
- Registration of property or legal documents
- Civil recovery of money or dues

Property and Inheritance:
- Legal heirs have rights to inherit parental property.
- Property inheritance can be through a will or according to succession laws.
- Legal heir certificates may be required for property transfer.
- Disputes can be resolved through civil courts.

Rental and Housing Issues:
- Tenants have the right to proper notice before eviction.
- Rent agreements define rights and obligations of both parties.
- Complaints can be raised before rent control authorities or civil courts.

Small Civil Disputes:
- Civil disputes usually involve notices, documentation, and filing cases in civil courts.
- Many issues can be resolved through legal notices or mediation before going to court.

General Guidance:
- Proper documentation is important in civil matters.
- Legal advice from a qualified lawyer is recommended for complex disputes.
