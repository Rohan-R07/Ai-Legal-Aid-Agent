Property disputes are common civil matters, often governed by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 and property laws.

Key Points for Property Disputes:
- Title and Ownership: The 'Title Deed' is the most important document establishing ownership.
- Injunction: If there is a threat of illegal construction or encroachment, you can file a suit for an injunction to stop the activity.
- Mutation of Property: After purchase, the property must be updated in the municipal records under the new owner's name.

Filing a Suit:
- The first step is typically to send a legal notice to the other party.
- A civil suit is filed at the appropriate court based on the property's location and value.
- Website: Case status can be tracked via the eCourts portal: https://services.ecourts.gov.in/
- Office: The local Civil Court (e.g., District Court or Sub-Judge Court).
- Form: The suit is filed as a 'Plaint' under Order VII of the Code of Civil Procedure.
